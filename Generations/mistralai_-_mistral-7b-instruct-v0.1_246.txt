The no retreat law requires individuals to attempt to retreat before using force against an attacker, even if doing so could potentially put them or others in danger. Proponents argue that it helps prevent unnecessary violence and promotes personal responsibility for self-defense. Critics, however, say it can create a situation where people may feel pressured into taking drastic measures rather than de-escalating a conflict.
