The Daily Mail is an English national daily conservative newspaper first published in 1896 . It was owned by the Barry family until 1993, when it was purchased by the David and Frederick Barclay, two twin brothers from the United Kingdom. It is currently Britain's best-selling daily newspaper, with an average daily circulation of 2,047,259 copies as of December 2015 . The Mail enjoys a conservative political orientation, and has been described by The Guardian as "the most right - wing national paper in the world". In 1986 it was revealed that Robert Maxwell had acquired a substantial stake in the paper.
