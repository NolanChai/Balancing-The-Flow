Seven U.S. Marines were killed and one other person was injured when two U.S. military helicopters collided in midair along the Arizona-California border on Thursday, officials said. The collision occurred near Yuma, Ariz., while the CH-53E Super Stallion and the MV-22 Osprey were conducting a routine training flight. The identities of the deceased Marines have not been released at this time, but all seven were assigned to Marine Heavy Helicopter Squadron 461 based in Camp Pendleton, Calif. The injured individual was taken to a local hospital for treatment. The cause of the collision is under investigation.
