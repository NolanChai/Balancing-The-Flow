Hermanubius was not just an Egyptian deity; he is also attested to have been worshipped at Dion, Macedonia and Thessalonike, Greece. Furthermore, Hermanubis was a part of the Sarapeum in Carthage, North Africa (LIMC n. 15) during the Roman period. This suggests that this cult enjoyed some popularity outside its original home in Egypt.[24]
