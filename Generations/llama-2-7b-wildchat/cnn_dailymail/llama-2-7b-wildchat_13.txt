A former Syrian doctor who came to the UK on an NHS referral was today sentenced to nine years for having a stash of highly dangerous chemicals.Faris Al-Khori, 62, had a huge amount of potassium chlorate and aluminium powder - two key ingredients in explosives.
He had also found a book on how to make bombs and a collection of other manuals about firearms and ammunition.Al-Khori was arrested at his flat in Widnes, Cheshire, after police raided the premises following intelligence he had been stockpiling explosive materials.The chemicals were found in a freezer as well as a plastic bag hidden under his bed.
At Liverpool Crown Court today, Judge Robert Trevor-Jones sentenced Al-Khori to nine years after finding he intended "to kill or seriously injure another with an improvised explosive device".
He said: "You came here to this country as a result of the NHS referral scheme. You were brought over for medical reasons - you have a heart condition and your blood pressure was high."But, as I've heard, since coming to this country you've been using your time constructively in attempting to learn English so that you could communicate with other people."
The judge added: "But you weren't content with that. You were also busy building up a collection of bomb-making chemicals and recipes for making improvised explosive devices.
"You were going about your business on the internet, learning how to make these devices."
