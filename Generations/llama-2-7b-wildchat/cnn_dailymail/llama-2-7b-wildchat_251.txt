1. The alleged mastermind of the 2008 Mumbai terror attack which left 166 dead has been released on bail, Pakistani officials revealed. Zakiur Rehman Lakhvi was allowed to leave Adiala Prison in Rawalpindi after posting $5 million bond.
2. The decision came as a shock to India and it strongly condemned Pakistan's move, which it called "contrary" to the assurances given by its leadership.
3. The government of Prime Minister Imran Khan assured that he will remain in custody till the completion of his trial.
4. External Affairs Ministry spokesperson Raveesh Kumar tweeted: "We strongly condemn the decision of Pakistan authorities to grant bail to Mumbai terror attack mastermind Zaki-ur Rehman Lakhvi".
5. He further added, "Such actions are contrary to assurances given by Pakistani leadership for effective implementation of the Mumbai terror attack case judgement."
6. The tweet also mentioned that India reiterated its call for expeditious trial and justice in this case without delay or any obstruction.
7. Pakistan, on the other hand, denied all allegations made by India saying there was no political interference.
8. Prime Minister Imran Khan's spokesperson Firdous Ashiq Awan said that the government had given its "complete assurance" to India that Lakhvi would remain in custody till the completion of his trial.
