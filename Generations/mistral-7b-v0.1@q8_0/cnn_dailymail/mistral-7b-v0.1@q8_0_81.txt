In a statement, Ayotte spokeswoman Elizabeth Guyton said Wihby resigned earlier this week after being placed on leave while the investigation was ongoing. "Senator Ayotte's office is fully cooperating with authorities as their investigation continues," she added in an e-mailed statement. Wihby could not immediately be reached for comment on Monday. Ayotte, a Republican representing New Hampshire who served on the Senate Armed Services Committee, was elected to the US Senate in 2010. Wihby was her chief of staff from January 2011 until he became senior adviser for constituent services in 2014, according to his LinkedIn profile.
