(CNN)Sunday's announcement that Corinthian Colleges Inc. would shut down all of its remaining 28 campuses is a positive development in a long struggle to hold for-profit colleges accountable. Corinthian was an infamous operator of for-profit colleges and vocational schools across the United States. It was the focus of investigations by state attorneys general and federal regulators for years before its collapse, and it's now the target of several lawsuits filed on behalf of students who allege they were defrauded. The shutdown comes after the U.S. Department of Education ruled in July that Corinthian violated federal laws by misrepresenting job placement rates to prospective students.
