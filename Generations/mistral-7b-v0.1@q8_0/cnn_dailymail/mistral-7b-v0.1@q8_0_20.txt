Kerr fled to the Midlands and then headed north before police found him at an address in Hull on Friday. Officers are working with Northumbria Police, where he was arrested, to decide whether he will be sent back to face justice in Leicestershire. A spokeswoman for Lincolnshire Police said Kerr had been arrested as part of Operation Evolve. This is a force-wide operation designed to identify and apprehend offenders who abscond from the police custody. The force’s chief superintendent Paul Timmins, who leads on missing people and sexual exploitation, said: “The officers involved in this investigation worked tirelessly to locate Kerr and bring him to justice.”
