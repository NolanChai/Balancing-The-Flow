
Harold Llewellyn Twite was an English soldier who served in the First World War, and his legacy continues to be remembered through his stopped watch, pipe, and other last possessions. These items serve as a reminder of the bravery and sacrifice he made for his country during this time of conflict.

Twite's stopped watch was found among his belongings after he was killed in action on November 4th, 1916. The watch is a symbol of the passing of time and the fleeting nature of life. It reminds us that every moment is precious and should be cherished. Twite's pipe also serves as a reminder of him and his courage during his time in battle. These items are now on display in a museum dedicated to First World War soldiers, ensuring that their memory lives on through the years.
