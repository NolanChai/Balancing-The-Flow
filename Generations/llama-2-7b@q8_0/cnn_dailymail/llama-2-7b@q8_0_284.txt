And she told voters who will face a choice between Labour’s Ed Miliband and Prime Minister David Cameron: “My responsibility is to keep you safe.”
Mrs May added: “There are three things that matter most in this general election campaign – the economy, education and security. It is not enough for politicians to simply say they will keep us safe if we don’t know what they will do about it. The reality is that lives depend on it.”
She said: “The police cannot operate effectively without new powers to track people who pose a threat.” And she accused Mr Miliband of being “soft” on terrorists and crime, saying his Labour Party had previously opposed the creation of the anti-terrorist Prevent programme.
