 Mohammad Ali Malek is alleged to have sailed an overloaded boat out of Libya with thousands of desperate refugees on board - before turning off his engine, leaving them drifting at sea for days and watching in horror as they ran out of food, water and fuel, then dying one by one.
The 34-year-old captain has been charged with multiple counts of murder after the shipwreck which killed more than twice the number of people who died when the Titanic went down a century earlier. But he was still behind bars in Malta last night - and refused to be questioned by police investigating his actions that day.
