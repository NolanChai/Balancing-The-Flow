100 metres from Mount Sharp in Gale Crater on November 7 using the High Resolution Imaging Science Experiment (HiRISE).
The image shows Curiosity’s heat shield, which is painted white to reflect sunlight. It can be seen as a black spot in the lower left corner of the photo with the rover itself to its right.
“With this first view of Curiosity on Mars from orbit, we have been reminded once again how exciting it is that these two spacecraft are exploring different parts of Gale Crater together,” said MRO’s project scientist Richard Zurek.
