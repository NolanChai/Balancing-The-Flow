Marolf, 38, was part of a group of SEALs participating in a swimming assessment when he became unresponsive in the water. Navy divers immediately responded and began CPR, but Marolf was pronounced dead at the scene.
This is the second death in less than two weeks involving a SEAL during training. On April 21, a 34-year-old SEAL died after being pulled from a pool at Naval Special Warfare Command in Coronado, California. The cause of that accident remains under investigation.
