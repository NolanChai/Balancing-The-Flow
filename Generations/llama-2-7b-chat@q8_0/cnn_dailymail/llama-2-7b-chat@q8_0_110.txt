To celebrate the 25th anniversary of the Hubble Space Telescope tomorrow, Nasa has released an image of a fantastic tapestry of stars snapped by the orbiting observatory. The image shows a giant diffuse nebula, with swirling clouds of gas and dust, set against a backdrop of glittering stars.
The nebula, called the Carina Nebula, is located about 7,500 light-years from Earth in the constellation Carina, and is one of the largest and brightest objects of its kind in the galaxy. The image was captured by Hubble's Advanced Camera for Surveys, which has been used to study the nebula since 2007.
The image shows a complex web of dust and gas, with stars forming within the clouds and illuminating the surrounding material. The brightest regions are where new stars are being born, while the darker areas are where older stars have died and are being consumed by the nebula.
"This image is a stunning example of the incredible beauty and complexity of the Carina Nebula," said Paul Hodge, a Hubble program scientist at Nasa Headquarters in Washington. "It's a testament to the power of the universe to create and destroy matter, and it's a reminder of the importance of continuing to explore and study our cosmos."
The Carina Nebula is one of several objects in the universe that are being studied by Hubble as part of its 25th anniversary celebration. Other images released so far include a stunning view of the Orion Nebula, a collection of galaxies in the Abell 370 cluster, and a view of the Milky Way galaxy from a different perspective than ever before.
As Hubble marks its quarter-century in space, scientists are looking forward to continued exploration and discovery with the observatory. In the coming years, Hubble will study some of the most distant objects in the universe, including the first stars and galaxies that formed after the big bang.
"Hubble has been a game-changer for astronomy," said Michael S. Johnson, director of Nasa's Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore, Maryland. "It has shown us the universe in ways we never thought possible, and it has inspired new generations of scientists and engineers to explore the cosmos."
With its unparalleled view of the universe, Hubble has revolutionized our understanding of the cosmos and has led to numerous groundbreaking discoveries. From the discovery of dark energy to the observation of the most distant objects in the universe, Hubble has opened up new areas of research and continues to inspire scientists and the public alike.
