
Police: Couple Dies After Being Hit by Train in North Carolina on Easter Morning
A married couple was killed after being struck by an Amtrak train in North Carolina on Easter morning, according to police.
Derek Lowe, 38, and Tina Lowe, 33, were hit by the train around 9:45 a.m. local time as they stood on the tracks near the Lawsonville community in Davidson County, WSOCTV reported.
Police say the couple was standing on the train tracks when the Amtrak Silver Star train struck them. Emergency responders arrived at the scene and pronounced both Lowe's dead.
An investigation into the incident is underway, but police believe the couple may have been suicide. The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) will also be investigating the incident to determine the cause of the collision.
"This is a very tragic event, and our hearts go out to the family during this difficult time," said Davidson County Sheriff Tim Brackett. "We are working closely with Amtrak officials to determine the cause of this accident."
The incident caused disruptions in train service between Charlotte and New York City, as Amtrak crews worked to clear the scene and repair damaged tracks.
This article originally appeared on U.S. News & World Report.
