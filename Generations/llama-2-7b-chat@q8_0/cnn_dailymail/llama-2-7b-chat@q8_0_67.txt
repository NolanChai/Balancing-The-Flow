Medina police said they received a call from the grandfather after he realized his cash was missing and discovered it had been spent at a convenience store.
The child allegedly admitted to taking the money and said he had spent some of it on video games and other items, reports Fox 8 Cleveland.
Police are now investigating how much of the money was spent by the boy and how he managed to get his hands on such a large amount of cash.
Medina police Lieutenant James Raber told Fox 8: "This is an unusual incident. It's not every day you hear about a 13-year-old kid with $25,000 in cash. We want to figure out how this happened and why it happened."
