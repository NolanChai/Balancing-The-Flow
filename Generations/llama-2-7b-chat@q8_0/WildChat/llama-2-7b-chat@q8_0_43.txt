Sweden's constitution is a fundamental law that establishes the country's political, economic, and social framework. Here is a comprehensive summary of Sweden's constitution:
1. Preamble: The preamble sets out the principles of the Swedish Constitution, including the rule of law, democracy, and respect for human rights.
2. Form of Government: Sweden is a parliamentary democracy with a constitutional monarchy. The King or Queen serves as the head of state, but has a largely ceremonial role, while the Prime Minister is the head of government.
3. Legislative Power: The legislative power is vested in the Riksdag (Parliament), which consists of 349 members elected by proportional representation for a term of four years.
4. Executive Power: The executive power is exercised by the Prime Minister and the government, which are responsible to the Riksdag. The Prime Minister is appointed by the King or Queen on the advice of the leader of the majority party in the Riksdag.
5. Judicial Power: The judicial power is vested in the Supreme Court, which has the highest authority in the country's legal system. The Supreme Court has the power to interpret the Constitution and to rule on the constitutionality of laws.
6. Individual Rights and Freedoms: Sweden's Constitution guarantees a wide range of individual rights and freedoms, including freedom of expression, religion, assembly, and association. It also provides for equality before the law, the protection of privacy and property, and the right to social security.
7. Local Government: Sweden has a system of local government, with county councils (landsting) and municipalities (kommuner) responsible for providing public services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure.
8. European Union Membership: Sweden is a member of the European Union (EU), and is subject to EU law. The EU has supremacy over Swedish law in certain areas, such as trade policy and monetary policy.
9. Amendment Process: The Constitution can be amended by a two-thirds majority vote in the Riksdag, but any change must be approved by the King or Queen.
10. Human Rights: Sweden has ratified several international human rights treaties, including the European Convention on Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms. The Constitution incorporates many of these rights into Swedish law.
11. Language: The official language of Sweden is Swedish, but Sami, Meankieli, and Finnish are also recognized as minority languages.
12. Religion: Sweden is a secular state, with freedom of religion guaranteed by the Constitution.
13. Education: Education is compulsory for children between the ages of 7 and 16, and the state provides financial support for schools and universities.
14. Healthcare: Sweden has a universal healthcare system, financed through taxes and public insurance schemes.
15. Social Security: Sweden has a comprehensive social security system, providing income support, healthcare, and other benefits to citizens.
16. Foreign Relations: Sweden has its own foreign policy, but is also a member of the EU and NATO. The Constitution sets out the basic principles of Swedish foreign policy, including neutrality and non-alignment.
17. Defense: Sweden has its own defense forces, but the country's defense and security policies are coordinated with those of other Nordic countries through the Nordic Council.
18. Finance: The Constitution sets out the principles of Sweden's financial system, including the role of the Riksdag in budgetary matters and the protection of public finances.
19. Public Debt: Sweden is not allowed to accumulate public debt exceeding 30% of GDP.
20. EU Membership: Sweden is a member of the EU, and as such, it has contributed to the development of EU law and policy. The Constitution sets out Sweden's relationship with the EU, including the supremacy of EU law in certain areas.
