Here are some ways to fluently rephrase "Being in a doctoral program":

1. Engaged in doctoral studies: This term conveys the idea of actively pursuing advanced research and coursework as part of a doctoral degree program.
2. Pursuing a doctorate: This phrase is similar to "engaged in doctoral studies" and emphasizes the ongoing nature of the academic work involved in earning a doctoral degree.
3. In the midst of doctoral studies: This rephrased version adds more detail by highlighting the current status of the individual's academic pursuits.
4. Working towards a PhD: This phrase is concise and direct, conveying the specific degree being pursued (Doctor of Philosophy or PhD).
5. Doctoral candidate: This term specifically identifies the person as someone actively working towards earning a doctoral degree, often used in academic or professional contexts.
