2. Overfitting: Overfitting occurs when a model becomes too complex and starts to fit the noise in the training data instead of the underlying patterns. This can lead to poor performance on new, unseen data.

Reference: "Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Christopher M. Bishop (Section 4.3)

3. Underfitting: Underfitting occurs when a model is not complex enough to capture the underlying patterns in the data. This can lead to poor performance on both training and test data.

Reference: "Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Christopher M. Bishop (Section 4.2)

4. Regularization: Regularization is a technique used to prevent overfitting by adding a penalty term to the loss function that discourages large weights in the model.

Reference: "Machine Learning with Python" by Andriy Burkov (Chapter 3)

5. Deep learning: Deep learning is a type of machine learning that involves neural networks with many layers. These networks can learn complex patterns and representations from large amounts of data, making them effective for tasks such as image recognition and natural language processing.

Reference: "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville (Chapter 1)

6. Transfer learning: Transfer learning is a technique where a pre-trained model is used to learn features from a new dataset, reducing the amount of data and time needed for training. This can be especially useful in applications with limited resources.

Reference: "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville (Chapter 6)
