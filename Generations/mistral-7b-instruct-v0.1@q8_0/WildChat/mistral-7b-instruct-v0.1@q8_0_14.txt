To create a button that displays required values for you through a query box in Excel, follow these steps:

1. Open Excel and navigate to the “View” tab.
2. Click on the “Macros” button and select “Record Macro.”
3. In the “Macro name” field, enter a descriptive name for your macro.
4. Select where you want to place the button. You can click anywhere in the worksheet or choose from one of the pre-defined buttons on the ribbon.
5. Click on the “Button” button and draw the shape for your button using the cursor.
6. Right-click on the button and select “Assign Macro.”
7. Select your macro from the dropdown menu and click “OK.”
8. In cell A1, enter the query or text box that will display the required values. For example, you can type =IF(A2=1,"Value 1",IF(A2=2,"Value 2","")). Replace "Value 1" and "Value 2" with your desired values.
9. Press “Ctrl+Shift+Enter” to enter the formula for the entire column.
10. Click on the button you created earlier and the required values will be displayed in the query or text box.

Note: Make sure that you have the necessary data in column A for your formula to work properly. You can adjust the formula as needed to suit your specific requirements.
