1. Income Statement: This projection shows the company's revenues and expenses over a specific period, usually a year or a quarter. It helps determine the company's profitability by subtracting total expenses from total revenue.
2. Balance Sheet: This projection provides a snapshot of the company's financial position at a specific point in time. It lists all assets, liabilities, and equity of the company, helping determine its solvency and liquidity.
3. Cash Flow Statement: This projection shows how cash is flowing into and out of the company over a specific period. It helps determine the company's ability to pay its bills and invest in growth by analyzing its operating activities, investing activities, and financing activities.
4. Break-Even Analysis: This projection determines when the company will reach the point where its revenues cover all its costs. It helps determine the company's pricing strategy and profitability potential.
5. Financial Ratio Analysis: This projection uses various financial ratios to analyze the company's performance. Ratios such as liquidity ratios, solvency ratios, efficiency ratios, and profitability ratios help determine the company's financial health and areas for improvement.
6. Sensitivity Analysis: This projection tests how changes in certain variables will impact the company's financial projections. It helps identify potential risks and opportunities and allows the company to make informed decisions about its future direction.
