According to the report, there were 80 unprovoked shark bites worldwide in 2013, down from a high of 96 in 2007. The number peaked at 84 in 2005.
The fatality rate was 25% in the latest year studied, compared with 22% in 2012 and 24% in 2011.
A total of 36 people were attacked last year, including three who died from injuries they sustained.
Thirty-three attacks occurred off the US East Coast, according to the report, which was released by the university's International Shark Attack File and is based on reports from more than a dozen countries.
