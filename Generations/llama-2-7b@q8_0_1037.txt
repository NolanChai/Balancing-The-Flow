
The first lady has made a name for herself as an advocate of healthy living. She's been instrumental in increasing childhood nutrition, and she helped organize the Let's Move campaign to combat childhood obesity. But Wednesday she took her advocacy into new territory by focusing on fashion education -- specifically by hosting a reception at the White House that brought together students with industry leaders and icons such as designer Tom Ford, model Christy Turlington Burns, and Vogue editor Anna Wintour.
