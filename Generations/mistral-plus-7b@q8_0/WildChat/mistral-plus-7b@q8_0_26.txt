The Constitution of Sweden is a codified written constitution that serves as the fundamental law of the Kingdom of Sweden. It consists of 18 chapters and was approved by voters through a referendum on September 29, 1974, and came into force on January 1, 1975.
The Constitution sets forth the rights and freedoms of citizens, the structure and powers of the legislative, executive and judicial branches of government, as well as other fundamental aspects of Swedish governance. It also provides for the protection of human rights and the separation of powers.
