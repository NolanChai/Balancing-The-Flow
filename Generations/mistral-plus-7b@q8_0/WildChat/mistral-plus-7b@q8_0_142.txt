Longwood State University's varsity football team is an NCAA Division II college football program that represents Longwood University in Farmville, Virginia. The team has been a member of the Central Intercollegiate Athletic Association (CIAA) since 2014 after spending their first four years as a non-affiliated independent. The Lancers play their home games at the George W. Perkins Stadium, which holds about 3,500 spectators. Longwood plays its football games as a member of the CIAA Southern Division.

Longwood State University's varsity football team has been in existence since 2012 and played their inaugural season in 2013. The team plays in the NCAA Division II level, competing at the conference level with teams like Virginia State University, Shaw University, and Bowie State University. Longwood's first coach was former NFL player Darren Arbet who served from 2012 to 2016 before being succeeded by former Winston-Salem State head coach Connell Maynor in 2017.
