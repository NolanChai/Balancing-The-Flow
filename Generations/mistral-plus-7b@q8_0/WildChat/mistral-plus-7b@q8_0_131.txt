Sometimes the words we use in our sentences can be too dry and uninspiring. It’s like eating a meal without any salt or pepper – bland and unappetizing. To make our writing come alive, we need to add some flavor with words that will make it sound more human, more relatable, and more engaging.

One way to do this is by using descriptive language. Descriptive language helps you paint a vivid picture in the reader’s mind of what you are trying to describe. It can add personality and emotion to your writing, making it more compelling and enjoyable for readers. For example, instead of simply saying “The car was red,” you might say “The car was a bright, shiny red that caught my eye like a fire engine.” By using descriptive language, you can bring your writing to life and help the reader feel a connection with what you are saying.

Another way to make your writing sound more human is by using figurative language, such as similes, metaphors, hyperbole, or personification. These devices can help add flair and creativity to your writing, making it more memorable for the reader. For example, instead of saying “He ran really fast,” you might use a metaphor and say “He sprinted like a cheetah on steroids.” By using figurative language in this way, you can bring your words to life and make them stand out more to the reader.

In summary, adding some flavor to our writing with descriptive and figurative language can help us create more engaging and human-sounding content. By incorporating these elements into our writing, we can add a personal touch that will make our words come alive and resonate with readers.
