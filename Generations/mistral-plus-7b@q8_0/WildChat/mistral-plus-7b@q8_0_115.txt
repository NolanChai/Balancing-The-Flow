The Biblical Antiquities of Philo is a work by Philo of Alexandria in which he attempts to explain the origins and teachings of Judaism through the lens of Greek philosophy. It comprises five books: On Abraham, On Moses, On Joseph, On David, and On Solomon. In these works, Philo explores the relationship between Jewish scripture and Greek philosophy and presents his ideas on the nature of God, creation, and morality.

The Legend of the Jews (1909) is an extensive collection of Jewish folk legends compiled by Louis Ginzberg. The work consists of 7 volumes, each dealing with a different aspect of Jewish history and culture. It includes stories from all over Europe, Africa, Asia, and North America, spanning thousands of years of Jewish history. The legends range from biblical tales to modern folklore, and provide an invaluable record of Jewish religious and cultural heritage.
