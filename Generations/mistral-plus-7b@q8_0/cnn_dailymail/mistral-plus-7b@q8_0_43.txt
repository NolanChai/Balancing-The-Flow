They hope to sign agreements to export dairy products, medical equipment, solar panels, and other goods. Cuba has been eager to modernize its infrastructure with US technology. Cuomo's visit comes in the wake of President Obama's trip to Havana last month. It will be a major boost for proponents of expanding trade with Cuba.

Governor Andrew Cuomo, who is traveling with a delegation of state officials and executives, says the trip could benefit New York as well as the Cuban people. “The economic possibilities that exist in this relationship are quite profound,” he said, according to the Wall Street Journal. “If you’re looking for investment opportunities—particularly in areas where we have strengths like solar energy and agriculture—the potential is there.”
