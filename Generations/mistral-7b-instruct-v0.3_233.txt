 Martin Luther King Jr.'s famous "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom. The speech was delivered on August 28, 1963, at the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C., and is considered one of the most important and powerful speeches in American history.

In his speech, King called for an end to racism and inequality, and envisioned a future where people would be judged by the content of their character rather than the color of their skin. He also called for economic justice and urged Americans to come together as one nation, united in the pursuit of freedom and equality for all.

The speech had a profound impact on the civil rights movement and helped galvanize support for the passage of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965. It remains an enduring symbol of the struggle for civil rights and is still celebrated today as a powerful expression of hope and unity.
