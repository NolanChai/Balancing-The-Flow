USDA's Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS) has jurisdiction over genetically engineered organisms in the U.S., while the FDA has regulatory authority over human food safety aspects of these products. APHIS conducts a risk assessment to determine whether or not it is appropriate to regulate the GE product and what kind of regulations are required, if any. The results of this risk assessment may include such decisions as: no further action; voluntary labeling or notification; import restrictions; field testing bans; federal quarantine; or permitting.
