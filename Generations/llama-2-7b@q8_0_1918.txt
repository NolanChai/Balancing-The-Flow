2. The Secretary is also a member of the Cabinet, which is made up of the heads of various executive departments in the federal government.
3. The Secretary’s duties include coordinating economic policy, monitoring financial markets, advising on tax reform, and handling relations with foreign governments on international finance issues.
4. In addition to these duties, the Secretary also serves as Chairman of the White House’s National Economic Council and Chairman of President's Economic Recovery Advisory Board (PERAB).
5. The Secretary also has some oversight over the Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC), which is a group of regulators from many federal agencies who monitor large financial companies to prevent another system-wide collapse like the one that happened during the 2008 global recession.
