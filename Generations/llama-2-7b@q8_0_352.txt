Kentucky Fried Chicken (or KFC) is the world’s largest restaurant chain. It was founded in Corbin, Kentucky, by Colonel Harland Sanders in 1952 with a $200 loan and began franchising in 1957. As of November 2018, it operates over 22,641 outlets in over 135 countries and territories around the world, making it the second-largest restaurant company in the world after McDonald’s.
