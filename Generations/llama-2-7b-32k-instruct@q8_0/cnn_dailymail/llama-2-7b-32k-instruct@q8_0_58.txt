British boy, 7, falls to his death in French Alps during family ski trip A seven - year - old British boy has fallen 160ft to his death from a cliff in the Alps after becoming separated from his family on a skiing holiday. Carwyn Scott-Howell got lost on the slopes and left the piste at Les Gets, in eastern France, early Sunday morning, it is understood. After being unable to find him, police were contacted by his parents who had become concerned he was missing, and a search of the area around 8pm local time began. An airplane, helicopter and dog teams were deployed and Carwyn was found at about 2am local time on Monday morning in an area near to where he fell from a cliff. He was taken by ambulance to a hospital in nearby Annecy but could not be saved. A spokesperson for his family said they were devastated, adding that "Carwyn had been enjoying his holiday with his parents and sisters, and the accident was completely out of the blue". (UK)
