Revellers in the rural village of Gobariya in Jharkhand state, India, were seen burning their skin with the hopes of bringing them luck during a traditional festival on Wednesday. The men and women gathered to celebrate the Kajri Mela, where people believed they could cure various diseases and illnesses by roasting themselves over an open fire for up to 15 minutes at a time. Other believers claimed the ritual would bring them good luck. It comes as a group of naked Indians were seen dancing in front of an elephant, another Indian festival where some men have taken part in a 'sacred' dance which involves standing on their own testicles during a religious event that began 40 days ago
