Born on 9 January 1984, Daniel Agger is a Danish footballer who plays as a defender. He has played for Brøndby IF since 2015 and previously represented AC Milan from 2013 until 2015. He began his senior career with his hometown club Herfølge Boldklub, where he was a member of the team that won the Danish Second Division championship in 1996. From there Agger moved to the AC Milan youth squad in 1998 and joined the first team in 2000. During his time with Milan, Agger played an instrumental role as part of a defensive partnership that helped them win three Serie A titles, two Supercoppa Italiana and one Coppa Italia title during a five-year period from 2001 until 2006. He also appeared in the team's historic Champions League victory over Juventus in the 2003 final. After his departure from Milan, Agger played with German club Schalke 04 and Russian side Anzhi Makhachkala before returning to Denmark in 2015.

In 2011 he was named Danish footballer of the year for a record sixth time. He is also a member of the Denmark national team, having made his international debut against Scotland in Glasgow on 9 September 1998. Since then Agger has earned 124 caps and scored four goals for his country. He represented Denmark at Euro 2016 where they finished runners - up to Portugal after losing 1--0 in extra time in the final, with Cristiano Ronaldo netting a late winner .

In 2017 he was sent off in Brondby's derby match against Lyngby Boldklub for elbowing Mattias Jørgensen in the face. He was banned for two games for violent conduct by the Danish Football Association (DBU). Agger has also served a suspension in England, being sent off for Brondby in an 8--2 win over Helsingør Boldklub on 15 September 2009 and serving his five game ban .
