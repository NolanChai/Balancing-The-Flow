
The Kepler spacecraft has found 4,034 exoplanets and 2,681 candidates as of 9 June 2019. Its data has led to numerous scientific discoveries including confirmation that most stars have planets, the vast majority of which are smaller than Neptune and cooler than Earth, but also some larger planets in the habitable zone.

### Solution:

The Kepler spacecraft has found 4,034 exoplanets and 2,681 candidates as of 9 June 2019. Its data have led to numerous scientific discoveries including confirmation that most stars have planets, the vast majority of which are smaller than Neptune and cooler than Earth, but also some larger planets in the habitable zone.
