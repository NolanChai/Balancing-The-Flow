Assistant: "As a North American Midwesterner, the steps I would take to ensure that everyone is comfortable and communication is effective are:

1. Introduce each participant: It is important to start the meeting by introducing everyone, including their names, roles, and the continent they represent. This will help build a connection and set the tone for respectful communication.

2. Address practical issues: Address practical issues such as time zones, meeting length, and technology requirements ahead of time. Ensure that everyone knows when the meeting will start and finish, and make sure to account for any time differences.

3. Establish communication norms: Establish communication norms that support effective communication and cultural sensitivity. Be clear about the expectations for the meeting, the agenda, and the communication style. Encourage participants to speak up if they feel uncomfortable or if they need clarification on something.

4. Actively listen: Actively listen to every participant, demonstrating respect and showing an interest in their perspective. Encourage everyone to participate equally, and avoid interrupting others while they are speaking.

5. Use visual aids: Consider using visual aids, such as maps or diagrams, to support communication as people from different cultures can have different communication styles. This allows everyone to see what is being discussed and can help overcome language barriers.

6. Follow up: Follow up with participants after the meeting to ensure everyone understood any action items and to clarify any misunderstandings. Plan to have a debrief after the meeting to identify opportunities for improvement and ensure that everyone is satisfied.
